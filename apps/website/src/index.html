How it works

Three ways in, one record

Different places need different methods. A lecture hall is not a loading bay and neither is a Sunday school. All three produce the same timestamped, auditable record.

Bluetooth

Beacon proximity

A small beacon in the room. Phones in range are recorded automatically after a sustained presence window, so nobody queues and nothing interrupts the session.

QR

Scan to check in

A time-limited code, shown on a screen or printed. Useful as a fallback where beacons are not installed, and for one-off sessions and visitors.

Manual

Supervised override

Phone flat, forgotten, or broken. A supervisor can record attendance directly — every override is written to the audit log with who made it and why.

Who it is for

One platform, three very different rooms

Workplaces

Shift-aware clock-in and clock-out, configurable cut-off windows, per-department reporting, and exports for payroll. Managers can correct records; corrections are logged, never silent.

Schools and colleges

Class attendance without eating into teaching time. Per-course records, lecturer dashboards, and exportable statistics for eligibility and reporting.

Children's ministries

SafeChild handles the moment that actually carries risk: pickup. Each child is issued a single-use PIN and QR pass at drop-off, and is only released against a verified, authorised guardian.

Security and data

Built so the record holds up

An attendance record is only worth something if it cannot be quietly faked or quietly changed.

One device, one person

An account is bound to the first device that signs in. Moving to a new phone requires an administrator to approve the change, which is what stops one person checking in for another.

Separate database per organisation

Each organisation's data sits in its own isolated database schema, not a shared table filtered by a column. One customer's records are not reachable from another customer's session.

Your data stays yours

Organisations own their records. We process them on your behalf, we do not sell them, and you can request a full export or deletion at any time.

Every change is attributable

Manual entries and corrections record who made them, when, and the reason given — so a disputed timesheet has an answer rather than an argument.
